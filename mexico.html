Mexico City Trip Itinerary

Dive into a week filled with cultural exploration and vibrant life in Mexico City. This itinerary is designed to fit your evenings and weekends, perfect for balancing sightseeing with work commitments.

Week 1

Monday to Friday (Evenings)

  • Monday Evening: Explore Centro Histórico: Walk around the main square, Zócalo, and have dinner at a traditional Mexican restaurant. Don't miss the Palacio Nacional and the Metropolitan Cathedral.
  • Tuesday Evening: Chapultepec Park and Museum Visit: Head to Chapultepec Park. Depending on time, choose between the Modern Art Museum and the National Museum of Anthropology (open until 7:00 PM).
  • Wednesday Evening: Visit Coyoacán: This bohemian neighborhood is perfect for an evening stroll. Visit the Frida Kahlo Museum (open until 5:30 PM, but get tickets in advance), and enjoy dinner in one of the local cafés.
  • Thursday Evening: Art and Culture in Roma: Explore the trendy Roma neighborhood, known for its galleries, theaters, and architecture. Have dinner at a rooftop bar to see the city from above.
  • Friday Evening: Nightlife in Condesa: Experience Mexico City's nightlife. Start with dinner at a trendy restaurant and continue at a bar or club in this lively area.

Saturday

  • Teotihuacan Day Trip: Spend your day at the Teotihuacan pyramids, an essential archaeological site. Consider a hot air balloon ride in the morning if adventurous. Have lunch nearby before heading back to the city.

Sunday

  • Xochimilco and Frida Kahlo Studio: Start with a morning trip to the colorful canals of Xochimilco for a boat ride on a traditional trajinera. In the afternoon, visit Diego Rivera and Frida Kahlo's studio in San Ángel.

Week 2

Monday to Friday (Evenings)

  • Monday Evening: Downtown Shopping and Street Food: Explore local shops and markets in the Centro Histórico area. Try some street food, like tamales or elotes.
  • Tuesday Evening: Visit the Torre Latinoamericana: Go up to the observation deck for panoramic city views. Afterwards, have dinner at a nearby restaurant specializing in Mexican cuisine.
  • Wednesday Evening: Lucha Libre Experience: If it aligns with event schedules, catch a Lucha Libre wrestling match for a truly unique Mexican experience.
  • Thursday Evening: Polanco Neighborhood: Visit Polanco for luxury shopping and dining. Enjoy some of the best restaurants in the city.
  • Friday Evening: Paseo de la Reforma Walk and Dinner: Take a walk along this grand avenue, enjoying the Angel of Independence and other monuments. Choose a restaurant along the way for a relaxing dinner.

Saturday

  • Puebla Day Trip: Visit the historic city of Puebla, about two hours from Mexico City. Enjoy its renowned culinary scene, colorful architecture, and local crafts.

Sunday

  • Relax in the City: Spend your last day relaxing. Visit any spots you missed or revisit favorites. Consider a leisurely brunch, a visit to a local spa, or just enjoy some downtime at a café.
